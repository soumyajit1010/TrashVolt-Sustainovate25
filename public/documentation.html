What is Energy?

Energy is the power we use to do things—like light our homes, run machines, or charge our phones. It comes in many forms, like electricity, heat, or fuel. Most energy today comes from burning fossil fuels (coal, oil, gas), but these create pollution and greenhouse gases like CO2, which harm the planet.

What is Waste?

Waste is anything we throw away—food scraps, plastic, paper, or old stuff. It piles up in landfills, releases methane (a strong greenhouse gas), and takes up space. TrashVolt tracks three types:

  • Organic: Food, yard waste—stuff that rots.
  • Recyclable: Paper, plastic, metal—things we can reuse.
  • Non-recyclable: Waste that can’t be reused easily, like some plastics.

How Do We Convert Waste to Energy?

Waste-to-energy (WTE) turns trash into power instead of letting it rot in landfills. Here’s how it works:

  1. Incineration: Burn waste to make steam for electricity.
  2. Anaerobic Digestion: Bacteria break down organic waste into biogas.
  3. Gasification: Heat waste without oxygen to make syngas for energy.
  4. Recycling Savings: Recycling saves energy by reusing materials.

In TrashVolt, we estimate: 0.5 kWh/kg (organic), 0.2 kWh/kg (recyclable), 0.1 kWh/kg (non-recyclable).

Why It Matters

WTE reduces landfills, cuts greenhouse gases, and gives clean energy. 1 kWh from waste avoids ~0.5 kg CO2.

Fun Facts

  • A banana peel can charge your phone with biogas!
  • Sweden uses 99% of its waste—no landfills!
  • 1 ton of recycled paper saves 17 trees.

All About Waste

Types of Waste

Waste comes in many forms, each needing different handling:

  • Municipal Solid Waste (MSW): Everyday trash like food, paper, and plastic.
  • Hazardous Waste: Toxic stuff like batteries or chemicals.
  • Industrial Waste: Factory byproducts like slag or ash.
  • E-Waste: Old electronics like phones or computers.
  • Biomedical Waste: Hospital waste like needles or bandages.

How We Control Waste Generation

We can reduce waste with these methods:

  • Reduce: Use less stuff (e.g., reusable bags).
  • Reuse: Use things again (e.g., refillable bottles).
  • Recycle: Turn waste into new products (e.g., plastic into benches).
  • Compost: Turn organic waste into soil.
  • Educate: Teach people to waste less.

Problems, Causes, and Solutions

Problems: Pollution from burning, high costs, not all waste works.

Causes: Old tech, poor sorting, lack of funds.

Solutions: Better filters, sort waste well, government support.

All About Energy

Types of Energy

Energy comes in these main forms:

    Kinetic: Moving energy (e.g., wind, water).
  • Potential: Stored energy (e.g., in a battery).
  • Thermal: Heat energy (e.g., from burning).
  • Chemical: Energy in bonds (e.g., in food or fuel).
  • Electrical: Moving electrons (e.g., power lines).
  • Nuclear: From splitting atoms.

Detailed Information

Renewable Energy: From sun, wind, water—clean but weather-dependent.

Fossil Fuels: Coal, oil, gas—lots of power but dirty.

Waste-to-Energy: Turns trash into power, reducing waste and emissions.
